Success Criteria 1.0: Understand what the SMART acronym stands for.

SMART stands for Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, & Time-Based.

Specific: Make your goal precise and concise.

Measurable: You should be able to measure how close you are to meeting your goal.

Attainable: Your goal should be feasible. It doesn’t need to be easy, but it should be physically possible.

Relevant: Your goal should be related to what you want to improve or do.

Time-Based: Your goal should specify when it will be accomplished.

SMART goals help you identify what you want to accomplish, how you’ll do it, and when you’ll do it.

Now, let’s look at an example.

Example:

Check out Juan, a 14-year-old high schooler who lives in Los Angeles, California.

Juan dreams of being a professional musician. But he knows that first, he must learn to play the guitar.

Since Juan is in love with Taylor Swift, he decides to learn one of her songs.

So, Juan sets the following SMART goal for himself:

“I will practice playing “Shake It Off” by Taylor Swift on guitar for from 5:00 - 6:00 PM every day this week so that I can play the song without stopping by the end of the week.”

Juan’s goal is Specific. It identifies the specific song Juan wants to play.

Juan’s goal is Measurable. He can measure how close he is to meeting his goal.

Juan’s goal is Attainable. It is physically possible for him to play the guitar for one hour each day.

Juan’s goal is Relevant. He is striving to be a professional musician, and this goal puts him on that path.

Juan’s goal is Time-Based. The goal includes a deadline and the time that he'll practice each day.

Success Criteria 1.1: Understand why setting SMART Goals is beneficial.

Your brain can grow.

In fact, your brain is constantly evolving and changing as you live your life.

It’s important that you are in control of how your brain grows and evolves so that you can have the knowledge and abilities that you want.

SMART goals help us direct our own growth, so that we can pursue the knowledge and abilities that we choose.
