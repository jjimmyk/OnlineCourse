Module 1: Set SMART goals.

Learning Goal 3: Understand what makes a goal attainable, relevant, and time-based.

So we have a specific, mesurable goal. We're done, right?

No, not quite! Now, you need to ensure that your goal is attainable, relevant, and time-based.

Success Criteria 3.0: Understand what makes a goal attainable.

This part is pretty simple. If a goal is attainable, then you have the physical ability to meet it.

Importantly, the goals that you set do not need to be easy to accomplish, but they should be possible.

Example

Meet Alexis. She is a senior at New Orleans Central High School and dreams of space exploration.

She wishes to one day float in space, gazing at the Earth from afar.

Learn by Doing

Which of the following is an attainable goal that Alexis should set?

The correct answer is "B" because it is difficult, but physically possible, for Alexis to apply to four colleges that offer aerospace engineering.

In contrast, building a functional spaceship in one night might be fun to try, but it seems physically impossible.

Success Criteria 3.1: Understand what makes a goal relevant.

This part is also pretty simple. Your goal should be relevant to what you want to do, improve at, discover or accomplish.

Let's revisit Alexis and help her set another goal, this time to help her improve in Algebra class.

Example

Meet Alexis. She is a senior at New Orleans Central High School and dreams of space exploration.

Alexis also struggles in Algebra class. She failed her Chapter Two test last week, and she felt like giving up in that class.

But, Alexis' teacher reminded her that she needs the class to become an astronaut. So Alexis is determined to improve in Algebra!

Learn by Doing

Which of the following is a relevant goal that Alexis should set to understand the Algebra material?

The correct answer is "B" because Alexis' goal is relevant to her success in Algebra class.

Applying to colleges is great, but it won't help her to understand Algebra concepts.

Success Criteria 3.2: Understand what makes a goal time-based.

You've arrived at the last step of setting a SMART Goal! Woot!

"Time-based" means that your goal should specify when it will be done.

Time-based goals let you know when to check whether the goal has been accomplished and when to work on it.

Let's revisit Juan, the 14-year-old high school student learning the guitar from our first activity.

Example

Check out Juan, a 14-year-old high schooler who lives in Los Angeles, California.

Juan dreams of being a professional musician. But he knows that first, he must learn to play the guitar.

Since Juan is in love with Taylor Swift, he decides to learn one of her songs.

Learn by Doing

Which of the following is a time-based goal that Juan should set?

The correct answer is "A" because we are told the specific time that Juan allots to practicing "Shake It Off". Juan's knows to practice from 5 - 5:30 every day this week. Go Juan!

In contrast, option "B" does not specify when Juan will practice the song, making it difficult for Juan to ensure he has time to practice.
