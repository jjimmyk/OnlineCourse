So we have a specific, measurable goal. We're done, right?

No, not quite! Now, we need to ensure that our goal is attainable, relevant, and time-based.

 

Success Criteria 3.0: Distinguish between goals that are attainable and those that are not.

This part is pretty simple. If a goal is attainable, then you have the physical ability to meet it.

Importantly, the goals that you set do not need to be easy to accomplish, but they should be possible.

 

Example

Meet Alexis. She is a senior at New Orleans Central High School and dreams of space exploration.

She wishes to one day float in space, gazing at the Earth from afar.

Success Criteria 3.1: Distinguish between goals that are relevant and those that are not.

This part is also pretty simple. Your goal should be relevant to what you want to do, improve at, discover or accomplish.

Let's revisit Alexis and help her set another goal, this time to help her improve in Algebra class.

 

Example

Meet Alexis. She is a senior at New Orleans Central High School and dreams of space exploration.

Alexis also struggles in Algebra class. She failed her Chapter Two test last week, and she felt like giving up in that class.

But, Alexis' teacher reminded her that she needs the class to become an astronaut. So Alexis is determined to improve in Algebra!

 

Learn by Doing

Which of the following is a relevant goal that Alexis should set to understand the Algebra material?

 

Success Criteria 3.2: Distinguish between goals that are time-based and those that are not.

 

You've arrived at the last step of setting a SMART Goal! Woot!

 

"Time-based" means that your goal should specify when it will be done.

Time-based goals let you know when to check whether the goal has been accomplished and when to work on it.
